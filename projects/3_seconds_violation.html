What This Does

This system automatically detects 3-second violations in NBA games by tracking when offensive players remain in the paint area for more than three seconds without attempting a shot. I built a complete computer vision pipeline that combines YOLOv8 object detection with ByteTrack multi-object tracking, court geometry analysis, and finite-state timer logic. The system processes game footage at 31 FPS, achieving 89% recallâ€”an 11 percentage point improvement over YOLO-only detection.

Technical Challenges & Solutions

Challenge: Player Occlusion During Tracking

Problem: Players frequently overlap in basketball, causing YOLO to lose tracking and restart player IDs. This leads to timer resets and missed violations.

Solution: Integrated ByteTrack multi-object tracker that associates detections using motion prediction and appearance features. Maintains player IDs through 2-3 second occlusions. Improved recall from 78% (YOLO-only baseline) â†’ 89% (YOLO+ByteTrack).

Challenge: Camera Angle & Broadcast Variance

Problem: Broadcast cameras change angles mid-game, distorting the paint polygon coordinates and causing false positives/negatives.

Solution: Normalized polygon coordinates relative to court keypoints (free-throw line, baseline). Validated paint mask accuracy across 5 different NBA arena camera setups (Warriors, Lakers, Celtics, Heat, Bulls).

Challenge: Shot Detection for Timer Reset

Problem: Timer should reset when player attempts a shot, but detecting shooting motions is complex and adds latency.

Solution (Current): Uses heuristicâ€”timer resets when player exits paint area (assumes exit = shot attempt). Achieves 89% recall but has ~8% false positive rate.
Future Improvement: Add pose estimation (MediaPipe/OpenPose) to detect shooting motion and reduce false positives.

Future Improvements

  • Add Pose Estimation for Shot Detection

    Integrate MediaPipe or OpenPose to detect shooting motions and automatically reset timer. Would reduce false positive rate from ~8% to <3% based on preliminary testing.

  • Multi-Camera Fusion

    Combine data from multiple camera angles to improve detection accuracy and handle occlusions more robustly using 3D player localization and triangulation.

  • Offensive vs Defensive Classification

    Train a classifier to distinguish offensive from defensive players (jersey color + court position), only timing violations for offensive players in the paint.

    Optimize model for NVIDIA Jetson or Intel NUC to enable courtside deployment without cloud latency. Target: maintain 30+ FPS on edge hardware.
